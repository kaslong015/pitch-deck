Question 20

Evidence & Mineral Systems Framework

At Dorogis, we frame our lithium opportunity within the LCT pegmatite mineral systems model that links fertile granitic sources, fractionation pathways, fluid and melt transport along structures, and traps within late-stage pegmatite swarms.

Mineral Systems Components

1. Source: Fertile Granitic Magmas

The younger granite province of central Nigeria provides the fertile parental magmas with high fractionation potential for lithium enrichment.

2. Pathway: Structural Conduits

Long-lived crustal structures focus late magmatic fluids. Lineament extraction maps reveal shear zones and fault systems that channel mineralized melts.

3. Trap: Pegmatite Emplacement Sites

The LCT Belt hosts structural and lithologic traps that concentrate lithium within late-stage pegmatite swarms.

Multi-Scale Data Integration

Regional Scale: Earth Observation

We have built a national-scale Earth Observation foundation integrating multispectral and radar satellite data, digital elevation models, and aeroradiometric grids. These datasets highlight:

  • Circular to elongate granitic bodies
  • Radiometric K anomalies
  • Belts of muscovite-rich alteration
  • Lineament systems focusing magmatic fluids

Camp Scale: Machine Learning Targeting

We have converted the regional model into ranked targets using machine learning that fuses spectral mineral maps, lineament density, lithologic proximity to fertile granites, and topographic break analysis.

Target Ranking Results

The highest-ranked targets cluster along second-order shear zones and cupola positions on the margins of younger granites, consistent with pressure-temperature paths that favor LCT pegmatite emplacement.

Prospect Scale: Drone-Based Exploration

High-resolution drone magnetics and LiDAR define brittle structures, dilation zones, and pegmatite ridge patterns under shallow cover. Hyperspectral drone surveys map:

  • White mica Al content
  • Li proxy responses where resolvable
  • Accessory mineral halos (tourmaline, garnet)

Ground Validation

Soil and stream sediment geochemistry shows coherent anomalies in Li, Cs, Ta, Sn, and Be with supportive K/Rb ratios and Nb/Ta systematics pointing to highly fractionated LCT-type pegmatites.

Mineralogical Confirmation

Trenching and channel sampling across priority ridges returned mineralogical confirmation of spodumene and lepidolite in multiple bodies. Core logging from follow-up drilling confirms pegmatite textures, internal zoning, and accessory columbite-tantalite consistent with evolved LCT systems.

Production Validation

We have advanced beyond prospecting into early extraction and test processing. Since 2021, we have produced approximately 220 tonnes of lithium-bearing material, 135 tonnes of lead, and 180 tonnes of copper, with core confirmations supporting mineral presence across the broader system.

Critical Uncertainties & Deposit Potential

Critical Exploration Uncertainties

Uncertainty Description & Management
Granite Fertility Determining which younger granite intrusions are truly lithium-fertile requires detailed geochemical and isotopic characterization to predict pegmatite distribution.
Fluid Pathways The extent to which faults and shear zones controlled pegmatite emplacement requires quantification through drone magnetometry and detailed field mapping.
Grade Continuity Vertical and lateral grade variations across pegmatite swarms need definition through systematic drilling to determine economic potential.
Hydrothermal Overprinting Understanding alteration processes that may have redistributed lithium will distinguish high-grade primary from lower-grade metamorphosed pegmatites.
Model Scaling Calibrating machine learning frameworks from regional to deposit scale requires ongoing refinement with new drilling and core data.

We are addressing these uncertainties through systematic data collection, structural modeling, laboratory analysis, and field validation. Each uncertainty represents an opportunity to deepen our understanding of Nigeria's lithium-bearing systems.

Global Deposit Analogues

Our exploration opportunity aligns closely with well-established global analogues of Lithium-Cesium-Tantalum (LCT) pegmatite systems. These analogues validate our geological model and guide expectations for scale, grade, and structural behavior.

Primary Global Analogues

🇦🇺 Greenbushes, Western Australia

Scale: World's largest hard-rock lithium operation

Key Features: Fractionated granitic magmas intruding along regional shear zones and brittle-ductile structures

Relevance: Identical fertile granite sources, deep crustal fluid flow, and structural focusing mechanisms observed in our LCT Belt

🇨🇦 Tanco, Manitoba, Canada

Setting: Bird River Greenstone Belt

Key Features: Clear internal zoning with lithium-rich core and tantalum-bearing margins

Relevance: Matches our pegmatite mineralogical patterns and late-tectonic granitic intrusion relationships

🇿🇼 Bikita, Zimbabwe

Significance: Africa's oldest known lithium mine (60+ years production)

Key Features: Proterozoic granitic pegmatite belt with petalite, spodumene, lepidolite

Relevance: Demonstrates African LCT pegmatites can reach economic scale; similar tectonic regime to our Belt

🇺🇸 Kings Mountain, North Carolina

Setting: Shear-controlled pegmatite swarms

Key Features: High-grade spodumene in structurally localized dikes over several kilometers

Relevance: Our drone lineament mapping reveals identical shear-controlled corridors

Analogue Validation Summary

What These Analogues Confirm

  • Lithium-rich pegmatites are associated with fractionated granitic sources
  • Major crustal structures control emplacement and concentration
  • Late-stage magmatic differentiation is critical
  • All these conditions are present in our Nigerian project area

Scale & Longevity Potential

Greenbushes and Bikita demonstrate potential for large-scale, long-life production. Tanco and Kings Mountain show that similar systems can host both lithium and tantalum by-product credits, improving project economics.

Scientific Justification

Our integrated Earth Observation and geochemical data indicate the same mineral system architecture as these world-class deposits, validating our expectation of discovering multi-million-tonne lithium pegmatite deposits within the LCT Belt. These analogues provide the scientific justification and global context confirming that Nigeria's central granite province has the potential to join the world's leading lithium districts.
